What data we collect, and why

1. Application form (guild.guide/apply)

When you submit an application to join Guild, we collect:

  • Email address (required, used to contact you)
  • First and last name
  • Base city
  • Years of experience guiding, website, prior employers, tours offered, specialties, and any additional information you choose to share

Legal basis: Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR — processing is necessary to take steps at your request prior to entering into a contract (your application to join).

Recipients: the city admin responsible for your stated base city, who reviews applications. We do not share your application with anyone else.

Retention: approved applications are retained for the lifetime of your Guild account. Rejected applications are deleted within 90 days.

2. Server access logs

Our hosting provider records standard HTTP access logs (IP address, timestamp, request URL, user-agent) for security and abuse-prevention purposes. These logs are kept for up to 30 days and are not combined with any other personal data.

Legal basis: Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR — legitimate interest in operating the service securely.

3. No analytics or advertising trackers

This website does not use Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel, or any other third-party analytics or advertising tools. There are no marketing cookies and no profile-building.

How we store and protect your data

Application data is stored in our database hosted by Supabase GmbH (an EU-based service provider). Email delivery is handled by Resend. Both processors are bound by data processing agreements that comply with GDPR Art. 28. Data is encrypted in transit (HTTPS) and at rest.

Your rights

Under GDPR you have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you (Art. 15)
  • Correct inaccurate data (Art. 16)
  • Request erasure of your data (Art. 17)
  • Restrict or object to processing (Art. 18 and 21)
  • Data portability — receive your data in a machine-readable format (Art. 20)
  • Withdraw consent at any time, where applicable
  • Lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority (Art. 77). For Berlin, that is the Berlin Commissioner for Data Protection and Freedom of Information.

To exercise any of these rights, email hello@guild.guide. We will respond within 30 days as required by GDPR.

Cookies

This website does not set tracking, analytics, or advertising cookies. The application form uses no cookies at all. Your browser may store small amounts of state related to its own operation; these are not set by us.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy if our data practices change. The "last updated" date below reflects the most recent revision. Material changes affecting your rights will be communicated to applicants by email.
